Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is located in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India. It was built by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in 1632. The monument was constructed in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. It is made entirely of white marble and is famous for its beautiful design. The Taj Mahal is considered a symbol of love and devotion. It is surrounded by well-planned gardens and reflecting pools. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and attracts millions of visitors every year.

Petra

Petra is an ancient historical city located in southern Jordan. It was established by the Nabataean civilization around 300 BC. The city is famous for its rock-cut architecture carved into pink sandstone cliffs. Because of its color, Petra is also known as the Rose City. It was an important trading center in ancient times. The city remained hidden from the world for many centuries. Today, Petra is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a major tourist attraction.

Machu Picchu

Machu Picchu is an ancient Incan city located in Peru. It is situated high in the Andes Mountains at an elevation of about 2,430 meters. The site was built in the 15th century by the Inca civilization. Machu Picchu was abandoned and later rediscovered in 1911. It is known for its advanced stone construction without the use of mortar. The site offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains. It is one of the most visited historical sites in the world.

The Great Wall of China

The Great Wall of China is located in northern China and stretches across several provinces. It was built to protect the Chinese empire from invasions. Construction of the wall began more than 2,000 years ago. The wall stretches over 21,000 kilometers in total length. It was built using stone, brick, wood, and earth. The Great Wall includes watchtowers and defensive fortresses. It is one of the greatest engineering achievements in human history.

Colosseum

The Colosseum is a large ancient amphitheater located in Rome, Italy. It was built during the Roman Empire around 80 AD. The structure was used for gladiator contests and public spectacles. It could accommodate up to 50,000 spectators at a time. The Colosseum was constructed using stone and concrete. It represents the architectural brilliance of ancient Rome. Today, it is one of the most famous landmarks in the world.

Christ the Redeemer

Christ the Redeemer is a famous statue located in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. The statue represents Jesus Christ with open arms. It is situated on the top of Mount Corcovado. Christ the Redeemer was completed in the year 1931. The statue is made of reinforced concrete and soapstone. It is a symbol of peace and Christianity. From the statue, visitors can enjoy a panoramic view of the city.
